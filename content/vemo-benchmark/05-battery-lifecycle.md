# Battery Lifecycle: SOH, Degradation, Second-Life & Circular Economy

> Battery fundamentals, lifecycle emissions data, degradation management, and second-life opportunities — directly applicable to LAFA fleet operations.

## Source
VEMO Talks #3 (Lithium & Energy Transition) webinar. Speakers: Nicolas Oxenford (Head of BI, VEMO), Pablo Castellano (Co-founder, Batex — battery reuse in Colombia). Moderated by Nati Ma and Melisa Beitia.

## 1. Battery Fundamentals

- **Core mechanism**: Lithium-ion batteries are electrochemical energy reservoirs — ions move between anode and cathode through an electrolyte
- **Why lithium-ion won**: Lead-acid lacks energy density (too heavy for EVs); NiMH was transitional; lithium-ion matured from consumer electronics to automotive scale
- **Cathode chemistry defines performance**:

| Chemistry | Full Name | Trade-offs |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| NMC | Nickel-manganese-cobalt | Higher energy density, higher cost |
| NCA | Nickel-cobalt-aluminum | High density, used by Tesla |
| LFP | Lithium-iron-phosphate | Longer lifespan, lower cost, lower density |

- **Key advantage over lead-acid**: Li-ion can discharge deeply and recharge without permanent damage

> **[LAFA]** LAFA uses LFP batteries (Geely, JAC, GAC vehicles) — the chemistry most favorable for longevity in CDMX's mild climate (88-92% SOH projected after 4 years per LAFA's own analysis).

## 2. Lifecycle Emissions: The Myth Debunked

- **Manufacturing**: Yes, EV production (especially battery) has higher upfront carbon footprint than ICE — but the comparison is misleading
- **Why misleading**: ICE manufacturing has 120 years of optimization; EV manufacturing is ~15 years old — the gap will narrow
- **Operation displaces emissions**: Every km an EV drives displaces ICE emissions. Even in grids that aren't 100% clean, EVs emit less in almost all of Latin America
- **VEMO data point**: In Mexico, an EV emits roughly **one-third** of an equivalent ICE vehicle over its full lifecycle
- **Key insight**: EVs make the most sense in **high-utilization fleets** — a private car used twice a month doesn't justify the embodied carbon
- **Recyclability**: Batteries are **95-98% recyclable** (metals and minerals) at industrial scale — not just lab results

> **[LAFA]** DaE vehicles running 10-12 hours/day maximize the carbon displacement ratio. High utilization = fastest payback on embodied emissions. Strong narrative for sustainability-minded investors.

## 3. Battery Health & Degradation

### SOH (State of Health)
- Industry-standard metric for battery condition — starts at 100%, degrades with use and time
- **End-of-EV-life threshold**: ~80% SOH — reduced range makes battery impractical for vehicles (but still valuable for stationary storage)

### What Causes Degradation

| Factor | Impact | Controllable? |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Deep discharges (below 20%) | **Biggest controllable factor** | Yes — driver training |
| High temperatures / overheating | Accelerates chemical degradation | Partially — climate + charging management |
| Age (calendar degradation) | Degrades even when idle | No |
| Intensive charge/discharge cycles | Cumulative wear | Partially — charging protocols |

### Critical Myths Busted
- **"Charge only when battery reaches 0%"** is **WRONG** — degradation accelerates below 20%
- Batteries often exit EV service **well before 80% SOH** due to operational decisions, crashes, or fleet turnover — significant reuse potential remains

### Monitoring is Non-Negotiable
- Continuous tracking of battery behavior in operation is essential to maximize lifespan
- Without monitoring, you can't detect early degradation patterns or optimize charging protocols

> **[LAFA]** Direct operational implications:
> - Battery monitoring is Track A infrastructure (P4 on the ai-roadmap, Month 3-5)
> - Driver training on charging habits (don't drain below 20%) protects fleet asset value
> - LAFA pays ~$300 MXN/week per vehicle for charging — proper SOH management extends battery life and defers replacement costs

## 4. Second-Life Batteries & Circular Economy

### The Batex Model (Colombia)
- Takes EV batteries exiting service → diagnoses SOH → reconfigures for **stationary energy storage** (solar complement)
- **Why it works**: Stationary storage has no weight/volume constraints — a battery at 80% SOH that's too degraded for an EV works perfectly in a utility room
- **Economics**: Batex's reuse-based storage systems are **~30% cheaper** than import-dependent new battery systems in Colombia

### Three Rs Applied
1. **Reduce**: Repair batteries (especially light EVs: e-bikes, scooters) to extend first life
2. **Reuse**: EV battery → solar storage (one battery, two industries served)
3. **Recycle**: Partnership with Altero (Colombia's only Li-ion recycler) — 95-99% material recovery at industrial scale

### Mining Displacement
- Every reused battery eliminates the need to mine raw materials for a new stationary storage battery — "mining-free batteries"
- Vision: Colombia as first LatAm country with full battery circularity; model replicable across region

> **[LAFA]** As LAFA scales to 2,000 vehicles, it will generate a meaningful stream of end-of-life batteries. A Batex-style partnership (or in-house second-life program) could become a revenue line and sustainability differentiator.

## 5. Lithium Extraction & Social Impact

- Lithium-ion batteries are **essential** for the energy transition — no viable alternative exists at scale
- Resources concentrated in sensitive areas: Lithium Triangle (Argentina, Chile, Bolivia), plus copper/nickel in Colombia, Indonesia, Congo
- "There will be some level of impact on communities. The question is whether we approach it as we always have (disastrously) or find new ways"
- Circular economy reduces extraction demand — not a full solution, but meaningful
- **Greenwashing risk**: Partial lifecycle analysis leads to false conclusions. Full 360-degree assessment required before claiming sustainability

> **[LAFA]** Understanding battery material provenance could become relevant for ESG reporting and investor due diligence as the company scales.

## 6. Where EVs Excel (and Where They Don't)

| Context | EV Suitability | Why |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Cities — ride-hail, transit, logistics | **Excellent** | Short distances, existing electrical infra, high km/day, air quality impact |
| Long-haul freight, trains, ships, aviation | Poor | Li-ion lacks energy density; hydrogen may fill gap |
| Private cars (low utilization) | Marginal | Cost premium not justified by minimal savings |

- **Right-sizing matters**: Match solution to problem — how many km/day, how many passengers, what infrastructure exists
- Bogota and Santiago have the **two largest electric bus fleets outside China**
- Light EVs (e-bikes) have exploded in hilly cities like Medellin across all social classes

> **[LAFA]** LAFA operates in CDMX — one of the world's most congested cities. DaE + LTO for ride-hail is well-positioned: electrifies high-km urban transport without requiring individual car ownership.
