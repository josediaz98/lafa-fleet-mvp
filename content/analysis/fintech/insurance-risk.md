# LAFA: Insurance & Risk Management

**Last updated:** February 7, 2026
**Scope:** EV insurance premiums, Mexico insurers (Qualitas, GNP, HDI), claims technology (BDEO), crash detection (CMT), vehicle inspection (Ravin AI).

---

## 1. The EV Insurance Gap

EVs cost approximately **49% more to insure** than gas-powered vehicles (Insurify data), though the gap is narrowing. Key drivers:

- **Claim frequency:** EV claims run **17% higher** than passenger vehicles overall (LexisNexis 2024)
- **Repair costs:** Higher repair costs and longer repair times (15.6 days vs 12.7 for ICE)
- **Battery risk:** Battery damage alone can trigger total-loss declarations because replacement costs ($13,000-17,000) exceed the 70-80% vehicle value threshold

## 3. Claims Technology

### BDEO (Madrid)
The standout claims technology for LatAm:
- Works with **37+ insurers across Latin America** including 9 in Mexico (Chubb, BBVA, ANA Seguros, Zurich, Allianz)
- AI-powered visual intelligence remotely assesses vehicle damage via geolocated video and photo
- Reduces repair authorization time by more than half

### Claim Genius
- 95%+ vehicle coverage
- Explicit LATAM versions in local languages
- Subscription or pay-as-you-go pricing

## 4. Crash Detection

**Cambridge Mobile Telematics (CMT)** processes **1 trillion+ driving data points daily** across 25 countries:
- DriveWell Crash system detects crash severity, predicts injury levels, identifies fraud
- Enables automated FNOL (First Notice of Loss) within seconds
- AI models have helped insurers reduce crash rates by **up to 35%**

## 5. Vehicle Inspection at Check-In/Check-Out

**Ravin AI** (Israel, $30M raised, TIME Best Invention 2022):
- Uses standard mobile phones -- no dedicated hardware
- Detects dents, scratches, and chips, generating 360-degree condition reports
- Already operates with Buggy, a mobility company in Mexico
- Optimal solution for lease-to-own where tracking condition changes over time prevents driver disputes

## Implications for LAFA

- **HDI's "Amplia iDriving" is the most interesting insurance product** for LAFA -- telematics-based pricing could reward safe driving behavior, creating alignment between insurance costs and driver management.
- **BDEO integration for claims processing** would reduce friction for both LAFA and its insurance partner. Already proven with 9 Mexico-based insurers.
- **Ravin AI is deployable now** for vehicle condition tracking at DaE shift changes and LTO check-in/check-out. Mobile-phone-based means zero hardware cost. This is listed as a "deploy now" emerging technology.
- **Negotiate fleet insurance rates** with Qualitas or GNP. At 150 vehicles growing to 2,000, LAFA has leverage for volume discounts. The 49% EV premium gap should narrow with telematics data proving fleet safety performance.
- **Budget for higher insurance costs** in early unit economics. The 49% EV premium is a real cost that must be modeled into both DaE and LTO pricing.

*Insurance pricing reflects 2024-2025 market data. EV insurance premiums are trending downward as repair ecosystems mature and claims data accumulates.*
